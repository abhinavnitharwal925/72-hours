Chapter 13 — The Stop

I stopped for gas once.

That’s where I saw them.

Not driving. Not waving.

Just standing near an abandoned car.

Dad. Mom. Kid sitting on the curb.

They looked tired more than scared.

That’s what made it dangerous.

They asked if I was heading south.

I nodded.

They said Arizona.

I thought about you.

I said yes.

At the light, metal pressed into my side.

Gun.

They twisted my wrist until the band snapped.

Blood ran down my arm.

They drove off.

I stood there bleeding.

Bare.
